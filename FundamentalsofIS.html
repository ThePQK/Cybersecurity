Fundamentals Of Information Security

These are the pillars that has to be followed by organisations to develop security policies and systems that ensure information security. It is used as a basis to find vulnerabilities and solutions to fix it.



Confidentiality

Confidentiality ensures assests to remain private to a certain organisation and authorised members. It prevents leakage and access to data by unauthorised individuals such as hackers etc.

Integrity

Integrity ensures that assets are protected from unauthorised use and modifications. Assets are to remain consistent and accurate unless it is altered by an authorised individual.

Availability

Availability ensures authorised access to assests by the appropriate individuals. Information and data are of little worth to people if they cannot access it when they need it. Thus, availability is important for authorised people to have timely access to data when required and meaures are needed to protect the availability and accessibility of data.
