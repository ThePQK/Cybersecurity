Reconnaissance

Before any attack can be done, reconnaissance (Info gathering) has to be carried out to learn more about the target or organisation in order for high chances of success



Reconnaissance Methods


Passive Reconnaissance

Passive reconnaissance refers to the attacker having no direct interaction with the target / organisation or system. This means using open sources to gather info such as social media, news channel. The attacker does not communicate with the target or expose himself to the target in any way but rather find out more about the target in secret so the target will have no idea that they are being targeted.


E.g.: The attacker may find more about their target through social media as social media is a platform for a huge amount of information that can be retrieved such as email ids, phone number etc. Using this way, the target will have no idea that they are being observed.
Attackers may also monitor a network and the packets sent and received by the target. He maybe able to find out vital info from unencrypted packets such as passwords without even having to crack the passwords themselves

Active Reconnaissance

Active reconnaissance refers to the attacker having direct interaction with the target / organisation or system.

E.g.: The attacker may use social engineering to convince the target to divulge sensitive info such as bank account info, passwords etc.

The attacker may also send his own packets to a server to try get a response that contains info. However, it may trigger the intruder detection system of the network which will prevent any attacks from being successful.
