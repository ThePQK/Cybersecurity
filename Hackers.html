Hackers

A computer hacker is a skilled expert who overcomes a problem using their technical knowledge



Black Hat Hackers

Black hat hackers are hackers that uses their computer knowledge for malicious intentions such as breaking into systems illegally and abusing sensitive info for their own personal gain. They also exploit vulnerabilities to do harm to the system, commit cyber espionage, infect a user's computer with malware etc.

Grey Hat Hackers

Grey hat hackers fall between the catergory of white and black hat hackers. They perform both ethical and unethical hacking such as gaining access to a system illegally to find vulnerabilites but without any malicious intent and release zero-day vulnerabilities to the public. They may also hack into a system's without the target's permission. They hack into systems to bring attention to vulnerabilties so that organisations can fix it, or financial gain, or just for fun.

White Hat Hackers

White hat hackers are employed by an organisation or the government to legally hack into a system to find vulnerabilites thrugh vulnerability assessment and penetration tests. They secure and improve the security of the tested system by reporting back to the organisation so that the necessary actions are taken to fix any vulnerabilities and prevent any potential cyber attacks from attackers.

Red Hat Hackers

Red hat hackers are hackers that fights against black hat hackers. They commit black-hat style hacks against cyber criminals to bring down them aggressively, even if they have to launch a full-scale attacks against them. They only fight against black hat hackers and cyber criminals. They may also work with white hat hackers to prevent cyber attacks and they do not seek any reward or pay.
